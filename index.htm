It Depends.

Seriously, though, there are pros and cons. There is no right answer for everyone.

Pros:

  1. You walk away with a great understanding of C and C++.
  2. The knowledge gained from EECS 381 prepares you for many interview questions.
  3. It turns you into a good programmer.
  4. "The only people who donâ€™t benefit from 381 are people who are exceptionally good programmers to begin with, or have already been programming for a very long time."
  5. "In regards to how this class will help you beyond college:
    1. You will have mastery over pointers.
    2. You will begin to understand how to write code that is easy to debug and change.
    3. You will be introduced to design patterns.
    4. You will begin to understand Object Oriented programming."
  6. You will leave the class knowing the STL forwards and backwards.

Cons:

  1. You spend a huge amount of time and effort on the class—effort that could be best spent on other classes, activities, or simply on life.
    • A significant amount of time is spent on iterating your code style and design to satisfy Prof. Kieras's preferences rather than actually doing something useful.
    • You can pick up design patterns yourself and can gain intimate familiarity with C++ just by trying to understand compiler errors.
  2. OO design can be learned in situ and you'll have extra time to do whatever (see #1).
  3. "Many students are there for the prestige and not the content."
  4. The overwhelming focus on C++ in EECS 381 creates an environment where it's easy to lose sight of the forest for the trees.
  5. "Spending the semester working on a such project, such as an app you ship to the store, instead of drowning in the absurd course load, seems like a 1000x increase in benefit for a student's future."
  6. "It's boring."

These opinions have been scraped from various conversations online and off. Most are not mine.
